About Me

Discover my journey, passions, and expertise

Background

I’m Marcus H. Francisco, an Electrical Engineer from Buffalo, NY, currently pursuing my Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering at the University at Buffalo. My engineering journey began in my childhood, where I spent countless hours soldering circuits and exploring electronics under the guidance of my grandfather, a technology teacher. These experiences sparked my passion for problem-solving and hands-on innovation, leading me to pursue Electrical Engineering.

Education

I am currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering at the University at Buffalo, where I focus on embedded systems, IoT, and control systems. My coursework and research have allowed me to work on projects like distributed IoT networks, such as "BlueLink," a connection protocol I designed for embedded systems. I have also competed in national hackathons, such as Pinnacle 2021 in Dallas, and led a Digital Twin project, which earned the Russell L. Agrusa Award for Innovation and was published by the Association for Computing Machinery.

Interests

Beyond academics, I am deeply involved in the music community. As a professional French Horn player, I perform with the Amherst Symphony Orchestra, the American Legion Band of Post 264, and sing with the UB Choir and Chamber Singers. I am also an assistant music director for the Enchords A Cappella group. My musical background has given me a keen appreciation for precision, discipline, and harmony—values that also translate into my engineering work.

Defined Career Goals

My long-term goal is to merge my technical expertise in embedded systems with leadership skills by obtaining an MBA. I aim to work as an engineer before transitioning into academia as a professor. I also aspire to contribute to sustainable electronics through my work with NextFlex, a consortium focused on advancing Flexible Hybrid Electronics. My future research will continue to explore innovative solutions in smart systems and controls.

Work Experience

I have worked as an Electrical Engineering Intern at Tapecon, designing Flexible Hybrid Electronics (FHE) circuits for wearable technology and cold-chain logistics. My work has been showcased at global conferences in California and Berlin. Additionally, I have served as a research assistant at UB, leading the development of an Interactive Digital Twin Campus Prototype that integrates real-time sensor data into a 3D visualization.

Certifications & Leadership

I am an active member of Tau Beta Pi, where I have held leadership positions, including serving as an Executive Board Member. I have helped organize Tau Beta Pi’s Career Fair and resume book distribution, increasing students' exposure to employers. I also attended the Tau Beta Pi National Convention, where I engaged with engineering leaders and expanded my network.
