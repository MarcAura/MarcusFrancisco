Projects

  • BlueLink IoT Smart Home Automation (2024)

      • BlueLink Bluetooth & WiFi IoT Network with Android App: Fall 2024: Designed and implemented a distributed IoT network featuring ESP32 microcontrollers connected via Bluetooth and WiFi, integrated with a user-friendly Android application. The system enables real-time control and monitoring of various IoT devices, such as smart lights, fans, a smart thermostat, air quality monitoring, and a flood water detector. The project, developed independently, demonstrates communication between a central Wi-Fi connected ESP32 hub and Bluetooth ESP32 functional devices, offering robust automation functionality through an app interface. The project involves mobile development, networking, and embedded systems, showcasing a scalable and modular solution for smart home automation. Project overview can be accessed via the following link: https://youtu.be/8Onl30TVQCg?si=hDx17WmNNWcEFkoD

  • 5G KPI Analysis under Varying Weather Conditions (2024)

      • Analysis of Key Performance Indicators in 5G Networks under Varying Weather Conditions: Fall 2024: Conducted an experimental study to evaluate the impact of weather conditions on 5G Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) such as latency, signal strength, and throughput. Developed a data collection system using a Raspberry Pi with a 5G hat, integrating real-time environmental data from WeatherAPI. Processed and visualized data with Python, Plotly, and Matplotlib heatmaps with interactive graphs correlating KPIs with weather metrics like temperature, humidity, and precipitation. The project shows resilience of 5G networks under adverse conditions, and offers recommendations.

  • Extended Capacity Uninterrupted Power Supply (2024)

      • Extended Capacity Uninterrupted Power Supply: Spring 2024: Developed a Deep Cycle Battery Uninterrupted Power Supply (UPS) system. This project is a senior design project with team members Marcus H. Francisco and Alexander J. Francisco, under the guidance of Professor Jennifer L. Zirnheld, Ph.D, at the University at Buffalo's Department of Electrical Engineering. This project aims to address the need for a reliable, cost-effective, and efficient UPS solution capable of sustaining long-duration power outages. This project procured two designs costing $502.80 and $681.74 capable of supporting a 600W load using a 100Ah battery, one model incorporating a solar-charging complication.

  • UB Hackathon - Red Alert Fire Prevention System (2020-2022)
