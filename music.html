Marcus Francisco

Professional French Horn • Vocalist • Music Director

Music as a Second Language

Music has been a constant alongside my engineering career, not a hobby held separately from it. As a professional French Horn player and vocalist, I perform with orchestras, concert bands, and choral ensembles in the Buffalo–Niagara region, and serve as Assistant Music Director for an a cappella group at the University at Buffalo. The precision, rehearsal discipline, and collaborative listening demanded by ensemble performance translate directly to how I approach complex engineering problems.

This page documents my active performing career, the mentors who shaped it, and the competitive milestones I reached as both a performer and a composer. Photos, recordings, and upcoming performance listings are coming soon.

Performing Ensembles

I currently hold active performance roles across orchestral, concert band, and choral settings.

Amherst Symphony Orchestra

French Horn

A full-size regional symphony orchestra serving the Amherst, NY community. Performs a season of classical orchestral repertoire spanning romantic to contemporary works.

American Legion Band of Post 264

French Horn

A community concert band directed by Matthew Cool. Performs ceremonial, march, and concert repertoire for civic and community events throughout the region.

UB Choir

Vocalist

The flagship choral ensemble of the University at Buffalo. Performs major choral–orchestral works alongside chamber repertoire from a wide range of historical periods.

Chamber Singers

Vocalist

An auditioned chamber choir at the University at Buffalo focused on refined ensemble singing across Renaissance, Baroque, and contemporary a cappella literature.

Enchords A Cappella

Assistant Music Director

UB's a cappella group, for which I serve as Assistant Music Director — arranging repertoire, leading sectional rehearsals, and contributing to artistic direction and vocal blend.

Mentorship & Artistic Development

My formation as a musician has been shaped by conductors who pushed me toward excellence at regional and state levels. Their direction produced some of the most competitive results of my early music career.

Dr. Stephen Shewan

Director of Bands — Mentor

Under Dr. Shewan's direction I earned selection to Conference All-State multiple times as both a performer (French Horn) and a composer — a rare dual qualification that reflects both technical mastery and creative voice. I also participated in the New York State Bandmasters Association (NYSBDA) and a range of other state-level events under his leadership. His conducting standards established the musical discipline I carry into every ensemble I join.

Matthew Cool

Director — American Legion Band & Community Ensembles

Matthew Cool has directed me in the American Legion Band of Post 264, community band performances, and concert appearances with the Amherst Symphony Orchestra. His leadership in community music settings has given me broad experience performing across multiple ensemble formats and audience contexts.

Competitions & Recognition

Competitive music events at the county, regional, and state levels are a significant part of my musical biography. These auditions demand both technical precision and artistic maturity evaluated by professional panels.

  • Conference All-State — Performer (Multiple Years)
    Selected multiple times as a French Horn performer at the New York State Conference All-State festival, one of the most competitive music honor ensembles in New York State.
  • Conference All-State — Composer (Multiple Years)
    Also selected multiple times as a composer, with original compositions programmed and performed at the All-State level. Simultaneous recognition as both performer and composer is uncommon.
  • New York State Bandmasters Association (NYSBDA)
    Participated in NYSBDA events under the direction of Dr. Stephen Shewan, performing alongside the most accomplished wind band musicians in New York State.
  • All Erie & All County Festivals
    Selected for county- and regional-level honor ensembles, serving as stepping stones to state-level recognition and conducted by area music educators.
  • New York State All-State Music Festival
    Performed with the All-State ensemble at the New York State School Music Association (NYSSMA) All-State festival.
